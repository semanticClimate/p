semanticClimate at International Open Access Week 2025

Open Access Week 2025 (OAW 2025) brings together communities worldwide to advance open sharing of knowledge. The event will take place from October 20–26, 2025.

It includes a global, community-led campaign to promote open access, open science, and equitable sharing of knowledge — through talks, workshops, policy dialogue, and collective action.

Friday, October 24, 2025

Time: 6:00 PM – 7:15 PM IST | 12:30 PM – 1:45 PM UTC

We participated in OAW 2025 by hosting a talk and a workshop on opening up climate knowledge and scholarly literature.

Our sessions explored how semantic tools can make research more transparent, equitable, and impactful — ensuring critical knowledge is available to all, not locked behind paywalls. The session title was "Open Science and Citizen Science with #semanticClimate: Open-Software for Knowledge Liberation."

Session 1 (Workshop): Corpus creation from open access repositories and their analysis with semantic tools

Time: 8:30–9:45 AM EST | 6:00–7:15 PM IST | 12:30–1:45 PM UTC

  • Introduction to the semantic tool by Dr. Renu Kumari, Program Manager, #semanticClimate
  • Hands-on demonstration by Ms. Udita Agarwal (BRIC-NIPGR), Dr. Renu Kumari, and Ms. Shaik Zainab (Anurag University, Hyderabad)

This session was a hands-on demonstration of the pygetpapers semantic tool for building a corpus of open access literature in a structured, machine-readable format for further analysis.

Applications of a semantic, structured corpus:

  • Training NLP models for Named Entity Recognition (NER) and automated summarization
  • Facilitating literature reviews — identifying research gaps and formulating hypotheses

Challenges this addresses as publication volume grows exponentially:

  • Bulk download of data in less time
  • Machine-readable format
  • Single platform to access data
  • Automated article retrieval from open access repositories
  • Updated record of literature

Session 2 (Talk): Who Owns Our Knowledge? An Introduction to #semanticClimate

This session explored the critical question of ownership in research and knowledge sharing, and how open access can democratize knowledge — with a focus on making climate and scholarly resources more inclusive, equitable, and accessible to all.
